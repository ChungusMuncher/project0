Garlic Mushrooms on Toast

Start your day right with these rich and creamy garlic mushrooms.


  • Ingredients:

  • A nice crusty loaf (if possible, otherwise normal bread is fine!) cut into thick slices
  • Around 500g of mushrooms, wiped with a kitchen towel to remove any dirt
  • 1 heaped tablespoon of butter
  • A handful of thyme leaves
  • 4 cloves of garlic, 3 chopped, one reserved
  • A drop of red wine/balsamic vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons of créme fraîche
  • Salt and Pepper
  • Small drop of olive oil

  1. 1. Prepare the butter

    Cut your mushrooms up into (relatively!) even slices. Put your hob onto a medium heat, and add the butter and olive oil. We use the olive oil to stop the butter from burning and turning bitter. We want the nice browned butter, not burnt!
    Add the garlic to the butter after around a minute. Allow it to cook, stirring constantly, for around a minute.
    Once the garlic butter is nice and frothy, add the thyme leaves in. Stir for around 30 seconds with a wooden spoon to allow the leaves to infuse with the butter, without burning them.

  2. 2. Cook the mushrooms

    Add the mushrooms to your butter mix. Season well with salt and pepper, and cook for around 5 minutes or so.
    Now's probably a good time to toast your bread. Pop the slices into a toaster. Once the toast is done, rub the reserved garlic clove all over the bread. This will add extra garlic flavour to the bread.

  3. 3. Finish and serve up!

    Add the wine (or vinegar), and créme fraîche. Turn the heat off and stir everything to combine. Spoon the mushroom mixture onto the toast, and serve up!

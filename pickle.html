Japanese Inspired Radish Pickle

Zingy and fresh. Great with a curry, or even as a sandwich filler!


Ingredient Quantity
Radishes Around 20
Carrot 1
Cucumber 1
Rice wine vinegar/Cider vinegar 200 mls
Salt 2 tablespoons
Caster Sugar 4 tablespoons
Lime juice Half a lime's worth
Black pepper A pinch

  1. 1. Prepare the veg

    Start by washing the vegetables. You'll probably want to peel the carrot. Cut the carrot and cucumber into matchsticks using a sharp knife. Try to cut around the watery core of the cucumber. For now, we want to try to keep our veg dry.
    Cut the radishes into fine rounds. Feel free to use as many or as little as you'd like.

  2. 2. Salt the veg

    Chuck all of that veg into a nice big bowl, and add your two tablespoons of salt. (Don't worry, this will be washed off later. We're just using it to draw the moisture out of the veg to make it easier to pickle!)
    Toss the veg in the salt, making sure it's all mixed in well. Set the veg to one side for 30 minutes.

  3. 3. Make the pickling liquid

    While you're waiting with bated breath for your veg to finish salting, you can get on with making the pickling liquid. Add your vinegar, sugar, black pepper, and lime juice to a small saucepan. Bring the vinegar to a boil for around a minute to dissolve the sugar. Set aside to cool. (It doesn't have to be room temperature, just not boiling hot.)

  4. 4. Pickle the veg

    After your veg has had some quality time with the salt, be sure to rinse it under running water to get rid of excess salt. Pat the veg dry with a tea towel, then pour the pickling liquid over the veg. Feel free to transfer it to a jar or something similar if you're going to be keeping it for a while.
    If the container is airtight, this pickle can be kept in the fridge for several weeks!
